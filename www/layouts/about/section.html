Since 2001, MIT OpenCourseWare has shared Open Educational Resources (OER) from MIT, creating new opportunities for millions of learners and educators, and helping launch a global revolution in free access to knowledge.

MIT OpenCourseWare continues to build on this foundation. With a new web platform, ever-growing content, and collaborations across the vibrant open education ecosystem, we're creating a world of more equitable and inclusive education for all.

MIT OpenCourseWare is a free and open publication of material from thousands of MIT courses, covering the entire MIT curriculum and used by learners and educators around the world.

Knowledge is your reward. Use OCW to guide your own life-long learning, or to teach others. We don't offer credit or certification for using OCW.

No enrollment or registration. Freely browse and use OCW materials at your own pace. There's no signup, and no start or end dates.

Made for sharing. Download files for later. Send to friends and colleagues. Modify, remix, and reuse (just remember to cite OCW as the source.)

The OCW@20 Vision

We've made great progress in our first 20 years - unlocking access to knowledge, helping launch the OER movement and opening the complete MIT curriculum to the world. And we'll keep growing and evolving.

We're excited to introduce our new platform - which you are using now - to bring smartphone users into OCW's global community…provide more powerful and insightful ways to discover just the content you need… and be a foundation for new features and learning experiences for years to come.

Here's what's in store in the months and years to come...

Empowering more educators to use OCW in their teaching, seamlessly integrating with their institution's learning management systems, and providing new content forms that can be easily remixed and adapted to any learning context.

Sharing emerging knowledge for big global challenges like systemic inequities, climate change, and the future of computing and artificial intelligence.

Expand rich media like video lectures, podcasts, interactive assessments, and new educational forms like augmented and virtual reality.

Showcase more project-based courses that center MIT's "mind, hand, and heart" approach to student-driven participatory solutions for real-world problems.

Launch new collaborations across the Open Educational Resources (OER) ecosystem to create the more inclusive learning opportunities needed to advance educational equity, building the capacity for learners with varied perspectives, preparation and voices to access, adapt, contribute and apply open knowledge in their own contexts.

Beyond Course Materials

OCW is known for sharing the notes, videos, problem sets, and more straight from the MIT curriculum. In addition, OCW has grown to include other types of content that complement its core publication of course materials.

  • OCW Educator: Instructor Insights

    The OCW Educator project is a faculty-inspired initiative designed to pull back the curtain on MIT teaching for their colleagues at the Institute and around the world.

    Educators start here

    Through this project, many OCW courses now include an “Instructor Insights” section, which shares the instructors' perspectives on topics of interest to practicing teachers, such as course design, active learning, and engaging learners.

    Find course with Instructor Insights
  • Chalk Radio Podcast {{ partial "simplecast.html" "a9349695-05d2-4150-bba6-271cbf875ba0" }}
  • Open Textbooks

    Open textbooks are transforming the educational landscape, and OCW is glad to have many among our open educational resources. When educators use open textbooks for their classes, they help keep down the cost of education, so more students can afford to stay in school, and pay for rent, food and other necessities.

    Open Textbooks
  • MIT Open Learning Library

    MIT Open Learning Library MIT Open Learning Library provides interactive course experiences, similar to MITx, that include auto-graded assessments that give you instant feedback and allow you to track your progress as you work your way through the subject matter. And like OCW, this content is always open and self-guided and includes no live support, discussion forum, or certificates.

President's Message

MIT President L. Rafael Reif

In 2001, the age of digital sharing was just getting started. Google was still in its infancy. January of that year saw the launch of a little project called Wikipedia. And then, in the springtime, MIT presented OpenCourseWare.

The idea for OCW came from MIT faculty: to give away MITs course materials to the world online. The decision to move forward with this bold idea belonged to MIT's 15th president, Chuck Vest. Institute leaders could not have known it at the time, but in creating OCW, MIT launched a quiet but profound educational revolution. It unleashed the global open sharing movement, helping to pave the way for the worldwide phenomenon of open digital learning.

In its first 20 years, OCW has offered hundreds of millions of individuals a new pathway to their future—a true gift to humanity. But in those same 20 years, OCW has helped society prepare for the challenges of its future, too.

Consider the vaccines for Covid-19. They may appear to be an overnight success, but the truth is, this “medical miracle” sprang from careful, deliberate scientific research—over decades. In the same way, society responded to the pandemic emergency with a rapid and radical shift to remote learning. That shift occurred in a matter of weeks, but what made it possible was two decades of transformative work in digital learning. From the beginning of that long, transformative journey, OCW has been a guiding light.

If you ask MIT people to name those moments when they felt most proud of the Institute, the birth of OCW is right at the top of the list. That is a wonderful legacy and an inspiring challenge, for the next twenty years and beyond.

Sincerely,
L. Rafael Reif
March 2021
